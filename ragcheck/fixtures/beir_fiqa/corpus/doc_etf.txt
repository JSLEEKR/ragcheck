Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) are pooled investment vehicles that trade on
stock exchanges like individual stocks. They typically track an index and
offer lower expense ratios than traditional mutual funds. Passive ETFs like
VOO track the S&P 500 index, while active ETFs attempt to outperform a
benchmark. ETFs are generally more tax-efficient than mutual funds because
of the in-kind creation and redemption mechanism, which limits capital gains
distributions.
