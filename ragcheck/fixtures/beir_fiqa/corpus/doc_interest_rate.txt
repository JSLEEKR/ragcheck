Interest rates determine the cost of borrowing money. Central banks adjust
policy rates to influence inflation and employment. When rates rise, mortgage
payments increase and savings accounts pay more. When rates fall, borrowing
becomes cheaper and asset prices tend to rise. The federal funds rate is the
benchmark in the United States; the ECB main refinancing operations rate is
the benchmark in the Eurozone. Long-term Treasury yields reflect expectations
of future short-term rates plus a term premium.
