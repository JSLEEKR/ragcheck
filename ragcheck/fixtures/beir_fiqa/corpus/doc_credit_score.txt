A credit score is a three-digit number that lenders use to assess
creditworthiness. FICO scores range from 300 to 850; scores above 740 are
generally considered excellent. Payment history is the largest factor
(about 35% of the score), followed by credit utilisation (30%), length of
credit history (15%), credit mix (10%), and new credit (10%). Paying down
balances and keeping old accounts open are two of the fastest ways to
improve a score.
