An option is a contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the
obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified strike price
before a given expiration date. Call options grant the right to buy; put
options grant the right to sell. Option premium is determined by intrinsic
value, time value, implied volatility, interest rates, and dividends. The
Black-Scholes model is the standard mathematical framework for pricing
European options. American options can be exercised at any time before
expiry.
