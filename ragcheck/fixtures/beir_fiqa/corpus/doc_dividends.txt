Dividends are periodic payments that a corporation distributes to its
shareholders out of earnings. Most US dividend-paying companies pay quarterly.
Qualified dividends are taxed at the long-term capital gains rate, while
ordinary dividends are taxed at the regular income tax rate. A company's
dividend yield is the annual dividend divided by the current share price.
Dividend reinvestment plans (DRIPs) let shareholders automatically buy more
shares with their distributions.
