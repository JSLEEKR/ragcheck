A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan in the United
States. Employees contribute pre-tax dollars that grow tax-deferred; many
employers match a portion of contributions. Traditional 401(k) contributions
reduce taxable income today but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as
ordinary income. Roth 401(k) contributions are made with after-tax dollars
and qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. The 2024 employee
contribution limit is $23,000, with an additional catch-up contribution
allowed for workers aged 50 and over.
